Exhibition Introduction

The World Peace Art Exhibition aims to promote understanding and communication between different cultures through artistic expression, encouraging artists and students to use their creations to express their thoughts on peace, hope, and the future.

This exhibition will bring together artworks from artists and students of different backgrounds, showcasing their understanding and imagination of the "World Peace" theme through painting.

Eligibility

This exhibition is open to artists and student art creators.

Artwork Requirements

  • Format: Painting: watercolor, oil, acrylic, illustration, or mixed media. Original artworks only. No prints or reproductions.
  • Size: Maximum size: 24” × 30”
  • Submissions: Each student may submit 1–5 artworks
  • Theme: World Peace

Digital Artwork Submission

Participants must submit a digital version of their artwork for review.

File Format

JPG or PNG. Minimum 300 dpi.

Photo Requirements

Image must be clear and straight. Photograph in natural lighting. Avoid shadows and glare.

Artwork Description

Must include: Title, Artist name, Medium, Size, Year created, Selling Price.

Jury Selection Process

All artworks will be reviewed by the Jury Committee.

Evaluation criteria include: Relevance to the theme, Creativity and originality, Artistic technique and composition, Emotional impact, Overall artistic quality.

Jury Committee

The jury panel consists of professional artists.

Yan Baoyun (闫宝云)

Artist

Yan Baoyun is a painter with an artistic career spanning over two decades, long engaged in oil painting and art education. His artistic journey began at the Panjiayuan Gallery in Beijing, and he furthered his studies in oil painting at the Tianjin Academy of Fine Arts. After immigrating to Canada in 2022, he continued his artistic creation and teaching in White Rock, actively promoting artistic exchange between the East and the West.

Junyi Ji / Alex Ji (纪钧译)

Artist

Junyi Ji graduated with a Master's degree from the Oil Painting Department of Tianjin Academy of Fine Arts and is a member of the Federation of Canadian Artists. His works have won awards in numerous international art exhibitions, and he has held solo oil painting exhibitions in Vancouver. He currently has an art studio in Richmond.

Artwork Packaging Requirements

To ensure proper exhibition display, artworks must meet the following requirements:

  • Artwork must be framed or on finished canvas
  • A hanging wire must be attached to the back of the frame or canvas
  • The hanging wire must be secure
  • Artworks must be wrapped in bubble wrap for protection
  • Frame corners should be protected
  • Package labeled with: Artist name, Artwork title, Contact information
